Recall Project Objectives

Grain - (one row) = One recall event 

1.How did the total number of recalls change from 2024 to 2025?
Year-over-year increase in recalls: 1,539 in 2024 to 1,576 in 2025 (up +37 recalls, about +2.4%).
This suggests recall volume is slightly trending upward,
so it’s worth monitoring whether this is early signal of a broader quality or compliance drift.

2.Did the most common recall class change between years?
Total recalls stayed basically flat from 2024 to 2025 (1,539 - 1,576, +2.4%), 
Class I recalls increased (667 - 770) and made up a larger share of all recalls (43% - 49%),
while Class II declined (805 - 718). Overall volume didn’t change much, but 2025 skewed more serious.

3.How did monthly recall trends differ between 2024 and 2025?
Across 2024–2025, there were 3,115 total recalls (about 130 per month on average). 
Recalls were highly seasonal, peaking in June 2025 (303) and bottoming out in November 2025 (63).
Year-over-year, the pattern shifted slightly: 2024 stayed flat from Jan to Dec (147-145), 
while 2025 increased from Jan to Dec (99-137, +38), suggesting a stronger back-half-of-year ramp in 2025.

4.Which states saw the largest increase or decrease in recalls?
California becomes the clear #1 recall state in 2025, 
The top five states with increases each added roughly 50–120 more recalls in 2025, 
showing that most of the growth came from a small group of states.
The top five states with decreases each cut recalls by about 60–140 cases,
pointing to strong year-over-year drops rather than small improvements.
Most states changed only slightly, while a few states drove the big gains and losses, 
meaning overall recall trends were shaped by outliers, not nationwide shifts.

5.How did recall status improve from 2024 to 2025?
In 2024, recalls were overwhelmingly terminated: 1,358 of 1,539 total recalls (about 88%). 
In 2025, total recalls stayed about the same (1,576),
but the mix shifted sharply toward active work: ongoing recalls jumped to 778 (about 49%),
while terminated fell to 703 (about 45%).
Net: volume is flat year over year, but 2025 has a much larger share of recalls still in progress,
suggesting a backlog or slower closure rate.

6.Did the leading recall reasons change year over year?
Across 2024–2025, recall volume is essentially flat slightly increasing in 2025, 
with 322 recalls in 2024 vs 332 in 2025.
The biggest drivers are pathogen-related contamination,
with the single largest recall reason being Listeria detected on food contact surfaces in 2025 (86 recalls) 
and Salmonella-related issues close behind (e.g., cucumber ingredient contamination leading to sushi recalls)


## Key Metrics & Definitions
- Recall Count: Total number of recall events
- Recall Class:
  - Class I: High risk (serious health consequences or death)
  - Class II: Moderate risk (temporary or reversible health effects)
  - Class III: Low risk (unlikely to cause adverse health effects)
- Recall Status: Current stage of recall activity (e.g., ongoing, terminated)
